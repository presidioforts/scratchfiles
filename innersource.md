# InnerSource Product Development: Sensitive Data Protection & LLM Integration

## Overview

This document outlines the structure and team members involved in the InnerSource product development effort focused on integrating Microsoft Presidio's sensitive data protection layer with OpenAI GPT to enable features like log analysis, coding help, and documentation assistance for enterprise developers.

## Project Structure

### **Product Owner (Jane Williams)**
- Responsible for defining the vision and roadmap for the product.
- Prioritizes features and requirements based on business needs and stakeholder input.
- Ensures the product delivers value to enterprise developers and aligns with the overall business strategy.

### **Product Engineering Manager (Mike Johnson)**
- Oversees the entire product development lifecycle.
- Ensures alignment with enterprise goals and compliance with internal policies.
- Coordinates with the development team and manages timelines.

### **Strategic Product Innovation Manager (Dug Williams)**
- Focuses on identifying innovative opportunities within the project.
- Works closely with the engineering team to explore new features and use cases.
- Ensures the product stays ahead of industry trends and meets user needs.

### **Infrastructure Lead (Sarah Thompson)**
- Leads the infrastructure setup and management.
- Ensures scalability, security, and performance of the product.
- Works closely with the product engineering manager to align infrastructure goals with the overall product strategy.

### **Maintainers (Emma Davis & Alex Smith)**
- Responsible for the ongoing maintenance of the product.
- Reviews contributions from the engineering team and ensures code quality.
- Collaborates with other team members to integrate new features and fixes.

### **Engineering Contributors (John Miller, Lisa Brown, David Wilson)**
- Actively contribute to the development of the product.
- Focus on coding, testing, and implementing features as directed by the maintainers and the product engineering manager.
- Participate in team discussions and provide input on potential improvements.

## Development Model

This project will be developed under an InnerSource model, ensuring that the product remains within the enterprise while leveraging open-source-like collaboration practices. The team will follow best practices for code reviews, documentation, and continuous integration/continuous deployment (CI/CD) pipelines to ensure high-quality outputs.

This document serves as a guide for the team structure and roles within the InnerSource product development effort. With a clear focus on innovation and maintaining high standards, the team is well-positioned to deliver a robust and valuable product to enterprise developers.
