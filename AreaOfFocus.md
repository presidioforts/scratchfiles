1. Stabilizing SCP Microservice Platform Operations:

Current Situation: Over the past several months, the SCP platform, which supports critical products such as EPL, EPL-X (CI), UCD, and Harness (CD), has experienced multiple P0 production incidents. These incidents were primarily caused by MongoDB performance issues and inconsistent service responses (HTTP 500, 400, and 203 errors). Platform reliability has been a concern, as the engineering team has been managing both product development and production operations, leading to operational instability.

Focus Area: Transition the SCP production platform operations from the engineering team to the platform service team, ensuring a dedicated focus on maintaining a stable and reliable production environment. The EE platform service team will take ownership of production operations, prioritizing the resolution of high-impact issues and critical defects to improve platform stability.

Knowledge Transfer and Platform Understanding:

Current Situation: To facilitate this transition, we have initiated multiple Knowledge Transfer (KT) sessions and working groups to gain a comprehensive understanding of the SCP platform’s architecture and implementation. These efforts have laid the groundwork for a smooth operational takeover.

Focus Area: Build upon this foundation by conducting thorough analyses of ongoing and recurring issues, prioritizing the resolution of critical defects throughout 2024 and early 2025. The aim is to eliminate P0 incidents and enhance platform performance through targeted, data-driven improvements.

Partnering for Observability and Proactive Monitoring:

Current Situation: The engineering team has been collaborating with the Observability engineering team and SCP product engineering to develop monitoring and alerting capabilities for the SCP platform. However, progress has been constrained by the dual responsibilities of product development and operations.

Focus Area: The EE platform service team will take ownership of Observability and monitoring initiatives for the SCP platform, partnering closely with both Observability engineering and SCP product engineering to ensure the implementation of comprehensive monitoring, alerting, and other Site Reliability Engineering (SRE) features. By 2025, the goal is to establish a fully operational observability platform that provides real-time insights and proactive alerts, enabling the mitigation of incidents before they affect production.


2. EPL-X Product BAU Training Initiative:
Current Situation: Starting in 2025, application teams will begin migrating their apps from EPL to EPL-X, and new apps under the Sunrise initiative will also launch on EPL-X. To ensure the BAU engineering team is fully prepared for potential incidents and support related to EPL-X, the SME team is developing a comprehensive training initiative. This includes creating detailed training documentation, hands-on sessions, and scheduling different batches to accommodate the team.

Focus Area: The training initiative will begin in October 2024, with the goal of having the BAU engineering team fully trained and prepared by 2025. This effort will equip the team to handle incidents effectively as they arise and provide a smooth transition to supporting the EPL-X platform.
