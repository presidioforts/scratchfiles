CIWAT Platform Transition Team Structure

The CIWAT Platform Transition Team, part of the Engineering Enablement Platform Service Group, is organized into two main teams with distinct roles and responsibilities for managing production support, platform improvements, and performance optimization.

1. CIWAT Breakfix and Operation Team

Purpose:
This team is responsible for handling day-to-day operational support, including breakfix tasks, incident resolution, and alert triaging. They ensure the platform runs smoothly by managing production incidents and supporting the infrastructure.

Key Roles and Responsibilities:

Breakfix and Incident Resolution:
Quickly address and resolve any issues or incidents that affect the platform, minimizing downtime and impact on production environments.

Alert Triaging:
Monitor system alerts, prioritize them based on severity, and take action to resolve critical issues promptly. Ensuring effective communication during incidents and post-resolution analysis is a key part of this role.

Platform Environment Management (PCF):
Oversee and maintain the CIWAT platform environments in Pivotal Cloud Foundry (PCF), ensuring stability across both production and non-production environments.

MongoDB Support:
Manage and ensure the optimal performance of the MongoDB databases used by the platform, ensuring data integrity and efficiency.

Runbooks and SOPs:
Create and maintain Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) and runbooks to provide a structured approach to resolving common issues.


Team Members:

Incident Responders

Breakfix Engineers

Environment Support Engineers (PCF Specialists)

Database Support Engineers



---

2. CIWAT SME and Production Support Team

Purpose:
This team provides subject matter expertise and strategic guidance to support the CIWAT platform during production incidents, while continuously recommending improvements to services and infrastructure.

Key Roles and Responsibilities:

SME Advisory and Strategic Recommendations:
Act as subject matter experts (SMEs) for the CIWAT platform, reviewing and providing recommendations to enhance platform scalability, reliability, and performance. They work closely with the breakfix team to identify areas of improvement.

Production Support Collaboration:
Assist in production incidents by providing expert knowledge of the platform, aiding in complex issue resolution, and helping the operations team solve critical challenges.

Code and Service Review:
Conduct regular reviews of the CIWAT platform's codebase, services, and infrastructure to identify potential improvements in performance, security, and functionality.

Continuous Platform Improvement:
Proactively suggest and drive enhancements to the CIWAT platform, including architectural changes, service optimizations, and performance enhancements.

Collaboration with Development Teams:
Liaise with development teams to ensure that feedback from production incidents is incorporated into future releases and updates. They also ensure any changes align with best practices.


Team Members:

Senior Engineers (SMEs)

Production Support Leads

Platform Architects

Service Improvement Specialists



---

3. CIWAT Performance Engineering Team

Purpose:
This team focuses on ensuring the platform operates at optimal performance levels, monitoring the system’s health, and providing recommendations to improve efficiency. They are tasked with performance management, capacity planning, and stress testing.

Key Roles and Responsibilities:

Performance Monitoring and Dashboards:
Set up and maintain performance monitoring tools and dashboards to track key metrics across the CIWAT platform, ensuring SLAs are met and performance remains within acceptable limits.

Capacity Planning and Load Management:
Ensure the platform can scale by conducting capacity planning and load testing, ensuring the infrastructure can handle peak traffic without degradation in service quality.

Performance Tuning and Optimization:
Identify bottlenecks and inefficiencies within the platform and make recommendations to optimize service performance. This includes fine-tuning the infrastructure, database queries, and microservices interactions.

Incident Support for Performance Issues:
Provide support during incidents where performance is impacted, working closely with the Breakfix and Operations Team to diagnose and resolve performance-related issues.

Proactive Recommendations for Performance Scaling:
Offer ongoing suggestions for scaling the platform in response to increasing load and traffic, ensuring long-term performance stability.


Team Members:

Performance Engineers

Capacity Planning Specialists

Infrastructure Optimization Experts



---

Summary of Team Structure:

1. CIWAT Breakfix and Operation Team: Focused on incident management, alert triaging, and platform environment support, ensuring the smooth day-to-day running of CIWAT.


2. CIWAT SME and Production Support Team: Provides strategic advisory and expert support, focused on continuous improvement, production incident resolution, and collaboration with development teams to ensure best practices.


3. CIWAT Performance Engineering Team: Focuses solely on the platform’s performance, optimizing its operation through monitoring, capacity planning, and performance tuning.




---

Benefits of This Structure:

Clear Separation of Roles:
Each team has a distinct function, reducing overlap and ensuring clear ownership of tasks and responsibilities.

Specialization:
With specialized teams focusing on operational support, subject matter expertise, and performance, this structure enables a more targeted approach to managing and improving the CIWAT platform.

Scalability:
As the platform grows, this team structure allows for easy scaling by adding additional team members to the respective areas (breakfix, SME, performance) as needed.
