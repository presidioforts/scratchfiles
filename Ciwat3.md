3. CIWAT Performance, Monitoring, and Observability Engineering Team

Mission:
This team ensures the platform’s performance and reliability by monitoring system health, identifying performance issues (especially in the MongoDB environment), and implementing critical monitoring solutions. Their goal is to ensure the platform operates efficiently and meets service-level agreements (SLAs) through proactive monitoring and system optimization.

Key Roles and Responsibilities:

Reliability and Observability:

Set up and maintain monitoring tools to track system health and performance, ensuring visibility into the platform’s infrastructure and services.

Focus on real-time monitoring of critical components, including the identification of key performance bottlenecks across microservices and infrastructure.


MongoDB Query Optimization:

Identify slow MongoDB queries that could be causing performance issues across the platform.

Work closely with the Database Engineering team to fine-tune queries and improve MongoDB performance.

Ensure efficient database operations by implementing performance improvements and query optimization techniques.


Critical Alerting Requirements:

Analyze platform needs to identify critical areas that require alerting.

Implement an alerting framework that ensures key performance and stability issues are addressed promptly.

Define thresholds and metrics for alerts that ensure the team is notified of potential issues before they impact platform availability.


Synthetic Monitoring:

Implement synthetic monitoring to simulate user interactions with the platform, allowing proactive identification of potential issues before real users are impacted.

Set up automated tests that check for key functionality and performance indicators, ensuring smooth operation even during non-peak hours.


SLA Adherence and Monitoring:

Continuously monitor platform performance against SLAs, ensuring that the platform remains reliable and responsive to business and user needs.

Provide regular reports on platform performance, focusing on key metrics such as response time, throughput, and resource utilization.


Capacity Planning and Load Testing:

Note: Given the nature of the PCF Cloud environment, traditional capacity planning and load testing may not be a mandatory requirement. However, monitoring system usage and performance trends to ensure scalability will still be important.

Collaborate with cloud operations teams to understand resource scaling and adjust performance strategies based on observed trends, without the need for extensive manual capacity planning or stress testing.


Incident Performance Support:

Work closely with the Breakfix and RCA teams during performance-related incidents, providing real-time insights and support.

Ensure quick resolution of performance issues, particularly those related to slow database queries, resource bottlenecks, or alert-triggered incidents.
